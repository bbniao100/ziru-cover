# Quality Checklist

Run this checklist before final delivery or review.

## Thumbnail Test

- The main hook is readable at mobile feed size.
- The cover has one first-read element.
- The user can understand the topic within one second.
- Small labels can disappear without damaging comprehension.

## Click Reason Test

- The cover promises a pain solved, result gained, mistake avoided, curiosity answered, or identity matched.
- The main image supports the click reason.
- The title is not merely descriptive; it creates a reason to open.

## Layout Test

- Image area, text area, and whitespace are clearly separated.
- Important elements align to a visible grid or intentional axis.
- The layout has hierarchy: primary, secondary, auxiliary.
- Decorative elements do not compete with the main hook.

## Chinese Text Test

- Chinese characters are correct and not generated as distorted pseudo-text.
- Line breaks follow meaning.
- The strongest word or phrase is visually emphasized.
- The main hook does not rely on tiny punctuation or small words.

## Platform Crop Test

- Key face, title, and object are safe from likely UI overlays.
- The cover still works when center-cropped or displayed next to other feed items.
- Margins are sufficient for mobile screens.

## Conversion Test

- If the goal is conversion, one trust or action cue is present.
- The conversion cue does not overpower the click hook.
- The design matches the account's positioning and does not feel like unrelated clickbait.

## Delivery Note

When presenting variants, recommend one primary option and explain the tradeoff:

- Click-first: strongest stopping power, may feel louder.
- Professional-first: higher trust and brand fit, may be less aggressive.
- Conversion-first: clearer commercial intent, may reduce casual clicks.
